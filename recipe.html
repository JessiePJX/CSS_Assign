What is TOFU?

Tofu (豆腐) is a soft and relatively flavourless food product made from soybeans.
Tofu originated in China. It is low in calories but high in protein and fat that it is an important source of protein in the cuisines of Asia countries. It also contains many important vitamins and minerals. Tofu's making process involves salt coagulants such as sulfates for separate the curds from the whey.

Materials

Ingredients

3 cups of dry soy beans
drinkable water
2 TBSP of Epsom Salto
(or 75ml of lemon juice)

Timing

12 hr total
about 1 hour active

Instruction

Step 1: Soak the Soybeans

Rinse the beans well and add them to a bowl of water. The beans should be covered by 2 in (5 cm) of water.
Let them soak overnight at room temperature until it split easily when squeezed.
Drain the beans through a sieve or strainer, and reserve the soaking water. Add more filtered water to the reserved water until you reach a total of 8 cups (2 L).

Step 2: Grind those Beans

Heat 5 cups (1.25 L) of the reserved water in a large pot over medium-high heat. While that water warms, grind the beans in the blender with 2 more cups (500 ml) of the reserved soaking water.

Puree the mixture until it looks exactly like a milkshake.

Step 3: Boil the Bean Mixture

Pour the blended mixture into the hot pot of water.
Cook the soybean mixture for 3–6 minutes, stirring the bottom frequently to avoid any burning. You’ll know it’s done when it starts foaming.
Quickly turn off the heat and stir to deflate. Keep an eye on this mixture; it can boil over easily.

Step 4: Straining the Mixture

Place a colander or mesh strainer in the pot. Line the colander with a pressing cloth, such as a clean piece of lightweight muslin. Then drape the edges of the cloth over the pot’s rim.
Pour the hot mixture into the pressing cloth. Pause when the cloth is full, and let the milk strain. Repeat.

Pour the hot mixture into the pressing cloth. Pause when the cloth is full, and let the milk strain. Repeat.
When all of the soy milk has strained, gather up the pressing cloth and twist the sides closed to form a sack. BE CAREFUL, IT'S HOT!
You can extract even more milk by opening up the pressing cloth, adding in 1/2 cup (125 ml) of water, and pressing it again.

Step 5: Coagulate Soy Milk to Tofu

Heat the soy milk with medium-high heat while perparing for the coagulants --- dissolve 2 TBSP of Epsom Salt in 1 and 1/2 cups of warm water or just use 75ml of lemon juice.
Heat the soy milk up to 70 °C. Gently add the coagulants into the soy milk; make sure not to stir too much.
Then, leave it there for 10 minutes.

Step 6: Mold the Mixture

Prepare a tofu mold.
MAKE SURE YOU PLACE THE MOLD IN A CONTAINER TO CATCH THE DRAINING WATER.
Set up the pressing cloth as it is shown in the image.

Add the mixture into the prepared tofu mold with a spoon.
Be careful not to spill it out.

Cross-fold the pressing cloth then put the lid on to close the tofu box.
Place a weight on the lid and wait for about 10 minutes to 1 hour based on how you want the tofu to be.

Step 7: Remove the Mold

Remove the Mold and the pressing cloth.

Done:)
